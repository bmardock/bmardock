# Intent-Driven Execution Pilot

---

## TL;DR (Leadership)

We are running a 1–2 week pilot to reduce rework and improve alignment.

Instead of trying to fully define solutions upfront, we will:

* define the outcome clearly
* build small, testable changes quickly
* validate against real behavior
* adjust continuously

**What this changes:**

* Less time spent debating plans
* Faster validation of direction
* Earlier detection of issues

**What stays the same:**

* Deadlines
* Ownership
* Accountability
* Delivery expectations

**What you’ll get:**

* Continuous visibility into progress
* Clear decision logs
* Measurable outcomes instead of assumptions

---

## Objective

Test a faster way of working that:

* reduces late-stage rework
* improves alignment
* increases speed to clarity

This is a **pilot inside our current Agile process**, not a replacement.

## Direction Brief (Kickoff)

Define this before starting. Keep it simple.

### Problem

What are we trying to change?

---

### Intent

What should be different if we succeed?

---

### Constraints

What cannot change?

---

### Success Signal

How will we know it worked?

---

## Example

Problem:
Users drop off during checkout

Intent:
Reduce friction during checkout

Constraints:
No backend changes

Success Signal:
Higher completion OR fewer hesitations

## Guardrails

### 1. Keep intent fixed

We are improving toward the same outcome
We are not changing the goal mid-sprint

---

### 2. Timebox exploration

Max 2–3 days per direction before deciding:

* continue
* adjust
* abandon

---

### 3. Always produce signal

Every loop must result in:

* something testable
* something learned

## Success Criteria

* Faster clarity on direction
* Reduced rework
* Fewer late-stage changes
* Decisions backed by evidence
* No increase in chaos

---

## Final Notes

This is not about removing planning.
